Nicotine sulfate

Nicotine sulfate, solid appears as a white solid. The sulfate salt of nicotine, an alkaloid obtained from tobacco. Toxic by ingestion or skin contact. Nicotine sulfate, solution appears as an clear colorless aqueous solution. Toxic by inhalation and may be toxic by skin absorption. Produces toxic oxides of nitrogen during combustion.

Applications & Uses :

1. Insecticide: Nicotine sulfate is used as a broad-spectrum insecticide effective against a variety of pests, including aphids, thrips, mites, and leafhoppers. Typically applied in the form of a spray. It acts as a contact and stomach poison for insects.
2. Agricultural Uses: Used on a wide range of crops including vegetables, fruits, and ornamental plants. Particularly effective in organic and sustainable farming where synthetic pesticides are avoided.
3. Veterinary Use: Historically used to control lice, fleas, and other ectoparasites on livestock.
4. Household Gardens: Used by home gardeners to protect plants from insect infestations. It is applied as a foliar spray.

Storage Notes :

1. Container: Use tightly sealed containers made of materials resistant to nicotine sulfate, such as glass or certain plastics.
2. Storage Conditions: Store in a cool, dry place. Avoid exposure to extreme temperatures. Keep the product in a low-humidity environment to prevent degradation
3. Safety Measures: Store in a well-ventilated area to prevent the accumulation of vapors. Keep away from food, feed, and potable water sources to prevent contamination.
4. Spill and Leak Prevention : Use secondary containment measures, such as trays or spill pallets, to catch any leaks or spills.
5. Regulatory Compliance: Follow all local, state, and federal regulations regarding the storage of hazardous chemicals.
6. Disposal: Dispose of according to local hazardous waste disposal regulations. Do not pour into drains or release into the environment.
